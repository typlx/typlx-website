Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Typlx offers two products — a Chrome browser extension and an Android keyboard. They handle data differently; both are described below.

Typlx Android Keyboard

How text is processed

When you tap "Fix Grammar", use the tone rewriter, or enable auto-suggest, your text is sent over HTTPS to the AI API endpoint you configure in Settings (e.g. OpenAI, Groq, a local Ollama server). Typlx does not operate this endpoint and never receives a copy of your text. The request goes directly from your device to the server you choose.

API token storage

Your API token is stored exclusively on your device using AES-256-GCM encryption (Android Jetpack Security Crypto / EncryptedSharedPreferences). It is never transmitted to Typlx servers.

Crash reporting

The Android keyboard uses Firebase Crashlytics to collect anonymous crash reports. When the app crashes, a report is sent to Google/Firebase containing the stack trace, device model, Android version, and app version. No text you type is included in crash reports. You can opt out of crash reporting in Settings › Privacy.

What we do not collect

  • We do not collect the text you type or submit for grammar correction
  • We do not use advertising identifiers or track usage behaviour
  • We do not require a Typlx account
  • We do not sell data to third parties

Third-party services

  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google) — crash reports only; see Firebase Privacy
  • Your chosen AI provider — text you submit for grammar correction is governed by that provider's privacy policy

Typlx Chrome Extension

The Short Version

The Chrome extension sends your text to the AI API endpoint you configure. It does not send data to Typlx servers or use analytics.

What we don't collect

  • We do not collect the text you type or check
  • We do not track your browsing history or habits
  • We do not use cookies for tracking or advertising
  • We do not share any data with third parties
  • We do not require an account or email address

Browser permissions

  • Active tab access — to read and check text on the current page
  • Storage — to save your preferences locally in your browser
